Speaking

I talk about potential — where it hides, what keeps it locked up, and what it takes to build the systems that release it. Every talk draws from seven years inside SpaceX, building a quantum computing company from scratch, running a gallery, and the ongoing experiment of treating all of it as one body of work.

Topics

The Operating System Behind the Rocket

What SpaceX taught me about potential. The technology powering the rocket matters less than the operating system powering the people building it — and what that means for how we build everything else.

Potential as Physics

Quantum advantage is real and locked in research labs. How QSS is building the full stack — cloud-scale quantum simulation and enterprise AI software today, deterministic optical hardware tomorrow — and what it looks like to build the container that releases potential at the level of physics.

One Body of Work

A quantum software and hardware company, a human potential company, an art gallery, a body, a performance practice. It looks scattered until you see the obsession underneath. How to stop building separate things and start recognizing the throughline.

The Cost of Seeing Potential

When you can see what something could become, the gap between what is and what could be is always visible too. The tension between "not yet" and "already enough" — and why staying in that gap is the actual work.

Book a talk

Available for conferences, panels, fireside chats, and podcast interviews. I don't do scripted pitches — I show up and think out loud. The search itself is the story.
