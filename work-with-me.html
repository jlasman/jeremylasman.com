Work With Me

Two ways to work with someone who sees potential for a living.

The Greatness Experiment

1:1 — scoped to the arena.

You already know the potential is there. You can feel it — in the work you're building, in the version of yourself that shows up in your best moments. The problem isn't seeing it. The problem is that something keeps getting between you and it.

The Greatness Experiment is a 1:1 engagement with its own architecture — not a program, not a curriculum, not advice. An experiment designed around what you're actually building and what's actually in the way.

The Arena

Every experiment starts by choosing your arena — the specific thing you're stepping into, the outcome you're aiming at. Not a vision board. Not a five-year plan. The arena is the space where you stop being a spectator of your own life and actively engage in the work of becoming.

Most people choose wrong here. They aim too big and dissociate. Too small and they stay comfortable. Or they pick what they think they should want — conditioned, automatic, borrowed. We find the experiment that's actually yours.

The Match

The Match is where your inner rivalry steps into the light. In one live session, we take a real source of heat in your life and turn it into a 3D pattern you can actually work with.

We start where there's charge now: a recurring conflict, a stuck project, a burnout loop, a relationship pattern, a fear that keeps hijacking you. Together we look at what's running you automatically — the sequence, stances, frames, voices, old logic, and unexpressed emotion — versus your actual conscious agency.

At some point there's an explosion of insight. People say things like, "I've never seen it this close before," or "I don't have to respond that way." That's the Match: you move from being the pattern to holding a 3D model of it, seeing how Survival vs Creation, Fear vs Love has been playing out in this specific corner of your life.

Before you leave, we design a simple experiment to run in the wild — one precise way you'll respond differently the next time this pattern gets triggered. Between sessions, reality becomes the testing ground, and your next Match builds on what actually happened, not on theory.

Testing & Execution

Then you go test it. New choices, new decisions, new moves — taken into the arena to see what happens. Here's the part most people miss: wins and losses don't define the experiment. A loss to fear still teaches you exactly where the edge is. The only thing that matters is this moment, this rep, this test.

There is no scoreboard. Every session is Day One. We don't reference past wins or coast on momentum. We meet the work fresh, every time, with the intensity of someone who knows they have to earn the next conversation.

The architecture goes deeper than what's on this page. There are more layers — how we map the people in your life who shape the experiment, how we surface the patterns that keep recurring, how we separate what's real from what's performance. That's what the container is for.

How long does The Greatness Experiment last?

The Greatness Experiment doesn't come in prefab lengths. It's one container that changes shape around the arena we choose. Sometimes the work wants a short, sharp run — about a month of focused contact around one decision or turning point. Sometimes it wants a whole season — a few months where we stay in the match long enough to feel what's really there. If you found me through a talk or a podcast, we usually start with a smaller experiment first, see what happens when you actually step in, and then decide together whether this wants to become a longer chapter.

The Main Event

One day. One person. Your location.

I fly to you — your city, your office, your arena — and we spend a full day inside the thing you're building. No slides. No framework handouts. No pre-recorded modules. Just a day where someone who sees potential for a living gives you their complete, undivided attention.

We start by mapping the real picture — not your pitch, not your goals doc, but the actual landscape of what you're building, what's in the way, and the patterns running underneath both. Most of the stuff that's slowing you down has never been said out loud. We say it.

What you walk away with

The one move

Clarity on the single decision that changes the trajectory — not ten priorities, one.

The pattern named

The specific thing you keep doing that you can't see from inside it — said out loud, made visible.

The decision made

Not a plan to make a decision later. The actual decision you've been circling — made before you leave the room.

This isn't strategy consulting. It's not therapy. It's what happens when someone who's spent their career inside rooms where potential becomes real sits across from you for eight hours and refuses to let you stay comfortable.

  • You don't need motivation. You need someone who can see what's already there and help you stop getting in its way.
